Refugee denied payout in deportation detention case

An Afghan refugee who was imprisoned by the state of Bavaria pending his deportation was denied compensation on Thursday, after a the Federal Court of Justice (BGH) overturned an earlier ruling that found his detention was illegal. The new ruling allows for different interpretations in how necessary pre-deportation detention is. Details of the case The man and his family came to Germany in 2013, having already applied for asylum in Slovakia. State authorities ruled they be deported to Slovakia and to ensure this, the man was detained in prison. His wife and baby daughter were housed in shared accommodation. A different state court later ruled the 27 days detention in the Munich-Stadelheim prison was disproportionate. On Thursday, the BGH found his detention was actually justifiable and therefore compensation was unnecessary. It also found that even if the jailing were unlawful, he still had no claim to federal compensation despite being arrested by federal police. Meanwhile, he was granted German refugee status after evading deportation through church asylum.
